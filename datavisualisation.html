Data visualisation

Data visualization is the visual presentation of data or information. The goal of data visualization is to communicate data or information clearly and effectively to readers. Data can be very difficult to wrap your head around, data visualisation can help you understand the data a little better. While it may be difficult to visualise data it does have a lot of advantages and can be used in many areas in business.

More on Data Visualisation:

Benefits:

Throughout business operation it has been a lot easier to spot trends in the market, particularly in the buying and selling of stocks in the business it allows for business to see potential threats and opportunities through the trends that have been laid out by data visualisation. Example: See a trading chart, that clearly shows all uptrends and downtrends of any business.

However one of the great benefits of data visualisation is the improved ability to spot all anomalies in the data that was provided, this makes it easier to perform to perform follow up questions in the delphi technique on all the outliers in the data that is currently held by the business or organization, with that a business can gain insight on some customer behaviour and preferences and in some cases even widen their reach to customers that are outliers.

Techniques:

  • Maps - Heat MAps, Google Maps
  • Charts - Pie Charts, Bar Charts,
  • Inforgraphics

Areas of data visualisation in business:

  • Business Intelligence and analytics
  • Journalism and storytelling
  • Scientific Research and discovery
  • Public Health and Communications
